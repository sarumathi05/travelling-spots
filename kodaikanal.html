KODAIKANAL TOURIST SPOTS

Bryant Park

Bryant Park is a botanical garden located in the heart of Kodaikanal. It is named after a British forest officer who planned the layout of the park in 1908. The park is spread over 20 acres and is home to a wide variety of flowers and trees. It is a popular spot for picnics and nature walks.

Kodai Lake

Kodai Lake is a man-made lake located in the center of Kodaikanal. It was created in 1863 by Sir Vere Henry Levinge, the collector of Madurai. The lake is surrounded by lush green hills and is a popular spot for boating and fishing. There are several shops and restaurants near the lake that cater to tourists.

Coaker's Walk

Coaker's Walk is a scenic promenade that runs along the edge of a steep slope in Kodaikanal. It was named after Lt. Coaker, who was instrumental in creating the walkway in the late 19th century. The walkway offers panoramic views of the surrounding hills and valleys, and is a popular spot for photography.

Pillar Rocks

Pillar Rocks are a set of three rock formations located near Kodaikanal. They are named after their resemblance to pillars, and are a popular spot for trekking and hiking. The rocks are surrounded by dense forests and offer breathtaking views of the valley below.
